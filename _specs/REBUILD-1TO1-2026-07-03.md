## TIMELINE PRINCIPLE PASS (v853) — "it used to just work / the battery-charge principle"
David's instinct was right: the timeline's correctness came from ONE law — **the battery: matte = potential · shining stripes = lived · the now-line = the conversion edge (brightest thing) that visibly moves; the past charges only where you LIVED it, the future is dim potential ahead** (REWARD-LANGUAGE.md). Point-fixes eroded it. **Archaeology:** the battery straddle core is old & worked (v461–472); the **v575 fix-wave ("2 agents, timeline cluster") bolted on the "render nothing below the now-line while tracking" special-case** — that's the disconnect. And the per-minute full-rebuild (`_lastMin` gate, [app.js:8971](app.js:8971)) made the charge **snap once a minute** = "everything looks static."
**Restored (v853):** (1) #3 already undid the v575 special-case — the committed future shows below the line again. (2) NEW `tickCharge()` — a per-second updater that moves the now-line + charged stretch (`.matchseg.chgseg`/`.calblk.live`) + committed future (`.committedfut`) **together** (reposition-by-dataset, same cheap path as the zoom relayout; ~1s linear CSS glide; suppressed during zoom/drag so no bounce). This is exactly the "every now-anchored element redraws together" the old creep-removal comment said was the right way — so it can't desync. **Preview-verified: over 3.2s all four move in lockstep (cfTop === nowlineTop), no console errors. FEEL (glide smoothness) = DEVICE-UNTESTED.**
