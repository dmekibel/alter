# David's Mantra / Self-Affirmation (his words) — for the Mantra-through-Song step + Self-Hypnosis installation

I have absolute trust in myself. My instincts are unerring, my thoughts are clear, and my feelings guide me wisely. I am the sole authority on what is best for me. I love myself unconditionally. Acknowledging my mistakes and imperfections, I embrace them fully and continue to love who I am. I hold deep respect for myself and have unwavering confidence in my abilities. I solemnly vow to keep evolving, never ceasing to improve, and seizing every opportunity to challenge and enhance myself.

I commit to pushing my boundaries and stepping beyond my comfort zone every single day. I will do whatever it takes to become a greater man, excel in my relationships, achieve greater success, and transform into something bigger and more profound.

I am an extraordinary person because I bring joy to others. My aura radiates positivity, and I am an eternal optimist. I am athletic, intelligent, loving, and ambitious, yet I savor life and cherish the beautiful planet Earth with all its marvelous little things that make me smile. I deserve the very best because I am a hardworking, life-loving, creative individual who is always a delight to be around. I am full of surprises, and people never tire of my company. I am the life of the party; my life is phenomenal—spontaneous and overflowing with love. I find joy within myself and don't take things too seriously.

I embrace every mistake I make and am grateful for them, for they are my greatest teachers. If something needs to be redone, fantastic—it's a new opportunity to craft it exactly as I envision.

I am not perfect, nor is anyone else. Everyone has their own challenges and moments of self-doubt.

I do not judge reality; I accept it. If someone doesn't resonate with me, that's perfectly fine—we're simply on different paths. That's interesting information to reflect upon.

I am indifferent to others' opinions of me. I am the master of my life, fully aware of what's best for me.

What would I do if I wasn't afraid?
