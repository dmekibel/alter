# Clever moves for ALTER's "open/awareness" focus: explicitly UNDO effort ("you can't DO resting as awareness"); name the seductive mind ("just understand this one last thing and then you'll rest easier" — don't take the bait); "pat the doer on the head, let it know it's irrelevant"; consistency ≠ duration, it's not being lured back into figuring-out.

Awareness isn't really being the watcher, and it's not really being the witness — both of those are things that egos do. And egos can get more or less skilled at being the witness or the watcher, or being more or less present. All that stuff is things the ego may or may not become good at if it really tries hard — but none of that is really what I'm talking about. Resting as awareness isn't a state of doing; it's a state of being.

You can't really "do" resting as awareness. If your mind would say, "Okay, that sounds nice — what do I do?" Awareness is already present. How do you rest as awareness? You don't *do* something to rest as awareness; doing something isn't even relevant. You see, resting as awareness is a state of being, not a state of doing. And so you also sort of surrender, or relinquish, the doer — that thing inside of you that's made up in your mind, that's always trying to get something right, understand it, do it correctly, wants to know "how do I do this, how do I do that, how do I perfect this?" That's the doer. And the doer is always asking questions — mostly questions like "how do I do that right?"

Well, the way you do resting as awareness is: you relinquish the doer. Pat it on the head, let it know that it's — what can you say — it's irrelevant. (Don't whisper it though; it doesn't like to hear that.) And just rest. So in order to rest, it's a letting go — and probably more accurately, it's a state of letting be. Because awareness, consciousness, is letting be everything about right now.

Your mind may or may not be in the position of letting everything be right now. Your mind may be trying to make certain things happen, or make other things not happen anymore — so your mind may not be letting everything be. In fact, most people's minds do not let very much be, for very long.

So in order to just rest as awareness, there's relinquishing the sort of neurotic need to understand, and it's also letting go of the neurotic need to do. There's a time for understanding, certainly, and there's a time for doing, certainly — but in terms of what I'm speaking of, those are the first two things that need to be relinquished: the doing, and the desire to understand. And then there can be a resting.

Now, when most people begin to rest, relax, acknowledge that this thing we're calling awareness or consciousness is fully present and functional — for most people it becomes difficult to rest for more than a few moments, because their mind keeps trying to entice them. Like a seductor or seductress: "No, no, no — see, if you really just understood this one last thing, then you would rest so much easier, you really would, I promise. Just think about this one thing. Have you noticed... just think about this one thing, solve this one thought, and then it'll be so much easier to rest as awareness." The mind does this often: "You did a nice job of resting for five seconds, that was nice, but you see — it could be so much better if you could just understand this, or understand that, or ask 'what do I do with it, and how do I do it, and how do I incorporate it into my life?'" You see what I mean? It's very — in one sense it's obvious, but it's also very subtle — that movement of mind that's always trying to understand, trying to do-do-do-do.

But you don't *do* awareness. That's why the first word I use is *rest* — rest as awareness — because the resting as awareness immediately gets your involvement out of the egoic mind state. It's not like you have to work a long time to get yourself out of the ego-mind state. Any instant you're resting as awareness, you can't be involved in the egoic mind state — the shoulds and shouldn'ts, the neurotic desire to grasp everything conceptually, on and on. You can't actually rest as awareness for even a split instant and at the same time be in the egoic mind state. So as soon as you rest, automatically, in that moment, you're not dominated, you're not caught in the egoic mind state.

Now, as I often tell people: if you did this one simple thing with any consistency, that would be amazing. If you could do this one simple thing with any kind of consistency — by that I don't mean necessarily you sustain it for a long, long time; that's not what I mean by consistency. I mean just not being lured into the mind trying to figure everything out, spiritually speaking. Because the hardest thing to do is to stay with one simple thing with any kind of consistency. It's easy to stay with something very complex — I could give a very complex, complicated, intricate, possibly interesting, maybe even entertaining spiritual teaching, and you could stay with that with such consistency, you could think about it for days or weeks or months. But to give anybody something extraordinarily simple — here's the teaching, and the method: rest as awareness — it's a little more difficult to have any consistency with that. Why? Because there's nothing for the egoic mind to do, and it gets a little nervous when there's not much for it to do, does it not? It gets a little unsettled. But nonetheless, that's the core teaching — and if you want to call it a technique, that's the core technique. That's it.

If resting as awareness actually happens — if it really starts to happen for you — then at some point it will dawn on you that you and awareness are not two different things. That you aren't actually somebody who's resting as awareness — that that was actually kind of a notion, an idea, an idea of you. That actually, awareness is much more what you are than all the ideas you ever had about yourself in your life.
